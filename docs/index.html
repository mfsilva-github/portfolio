Defining a Fictional Project

Let's establish a hypothetical opportunity scenario that will guide our project requirements:

  • ACME Corporation: A retail product seller
  • ABC Company: An IT service provider offering SaaS solutions
  • ElectricCar: A company selling electric vehicle charging stations

These companies all require a marketplace platform where users can purchase or subscribe to their products and services. These offerings should be customizable, with prices dynamically determined based on customization choices. Additionally, customers need a unified platform to manage their subscriptions, track costs, and monitor usage (if applicable).

Technology Selection in Real Projects

In real-world projects, existing infrastructure and employee skillsets significantly influence technology selection. A company heavily invested in on-premise virtual machines and with a team of Java experts might opt to build the solution using these familiar technologies. Migrating to new technologies can be a project in itself, often referred to as "digital transformation."

However, cloud adoption is a growing trend as companies seek to modernize their applications and capitalize on digital transformation. While aspects like microservices architecture and DevOps processes are crucial for digital transformation journeys, such business process changes fall outside the scope of this project.
